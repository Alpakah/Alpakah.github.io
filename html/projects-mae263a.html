5 DOF Robotic Manipulator | UCLA (2022)

I took MAE 263A: Kinematics of Robotics at UCLA in the Fall of 2022. This is a graduate-level class that I enrolled in as an undergraduate, which culminated in a final hands-on project in which we were tasked to build a minimum 3 Degree of Freedom (DOF) Robotic Manipulator to accomplish any task.

The team designed, manufactured, and operated a 5 DOF Robotic Arm Manipulator that had an additional 1 DOF gripping end effector to move to the known position of a cup, pick it up while maintaining its vertical orientation to avoid spilling, and pour its contents out at a final destination.

I established the DH parameters of the entire system and created the Forward Kinematic transformation matrix that would transform angular inputs to the Dynamixel Motors and output them as spatial coordinates in the base reference frame. I also designed the 4th and 5th links of the manipulator, as well as 3D printing all of the components out of a nylon filament.
