MAE Senior Capstone: Manuel

⇩

MAE Senior Capstone | UCLA (2023)

To graduate from the UCLA Samueli School of Engineering with a Bachelor's Degree in Mechanical Engineering, every student must complete a capstone course demonstrating all of their theoretical and technical knowledge by building a functional project. I had the fortune to complete my capstone under Dr. Dennis Hong, where students were able to pitch and pursue their own design for a mechanical assembly to demonstrate engineering skills.

Together as a team of 7 students, I created "Manuel":

Manuel is a manually controlled robot that was designed to perform gestures with its arm and spell in ASL with its hand. The arm was designed to mimic the full 7 degrees of freedom that a human arm has. The hand and fingers were designed to perform finger spelling, applying only enough degrees of freedom to do so. These objectives were to be completed within 5 months with a $600 budget plus part donations from the RoMeLa lab. Unfortunately, these ambitious goals were not fully met. Manuel managed to perform exactly one gesture with its arm and hand: flipping someone off.

I worked on the electrical system of Manuel, determining all the required voltage and current for all of the electrical components in the system and then choosing a sufficient power supply to provide enough power to the entire system without providing too much voltage or current to each individual component. I was able to re-purpose a 24V power supply from a broken 3D printer and step voltages/currents down to the required amounts; however, that would end up being extremely dangerous and produce high amounts of heat. I quickly learned that sourcing old 5V and 3V power cords would suffice, and I could splice and solder the wires to Dynamixel motors, Stepper motors, Arduinos, and LED matrices.

I also 3D printed every single part of Manuel, with both FDM and SLA printers. Thousands of hours using 3D printers came in handy, and this task was no problem at all.
