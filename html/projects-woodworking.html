Woodworking

At the beginning of my Freshman year of college at UCLA, I had access to woodworking power tools for a brief 6 months before the Covid Pandemic. During those six months, I spent a large portion of my free time doing woodworking projects because it's something I've always wanted to do but never had access to the tools for. I built pencil cases, a bookshelf, and even a music stand that I could hang on my bunk bed for me and my roomates to practice with sheet music.

Unfortunately, as I had just begun my studies in engineering, I did not have the thought to document or take pictures of most of these creations before they were thrown away. I do have a picture of the music stand, as well as a Jenga Case I built.

Laser Cutting with Wood

However, after being booted from the dormitories, I lost access to the residential Makerspace and transitioned to making more designs with the laser cutter in the Engineering Makerspace. I could no longer use thicker pieces of wood planks, but instead learned how to make more precise designs and assemblies. I used this skill to make signage and even the Dodgeball Trophy I go into more detail about in another Project Page.
